The limit

Even with intense training and simulations,
the human body has a gravitational force threshold.
A sudden and rapid change can constrict blood vessels,
preventing oxygen from ever reaching the brain.

The first to go haywire is the sense of sight,
crushing the little veins that goes round the eyes.
Followed by bruising in random places as they bleed inside,
With a short exposure one can recover,
but none may survive to defy this limit.

The crew of the USG Kellion,
each a veteran on space travel, fainted momentarily.
Awoken by the sound of cracking electricity,
they outmaneuvered death by a millisecond.

Hammond: "Is everyone okay?"

Kendra: "What...? What the hell were you thinking?! Were you trying to get us killed?"

Hammond: "I just saved our asses, Ms. Daniels! If we had aborted at that speed and distance, we would have smashed right into the side of the Ishimura. Now settle down, and let's get to work. Corporal, report!"

While Kendra and Hammond bicker in the foreground,
the blast shields retracted, showing the inside of a hangar.
Isaac put on his helmet and got himself ready to work,
knowing their shuttle sustained heavy damage from the fall.

Chen: "I'm not getting any readings from the port booster and we've lost comms and autopilot. It'll take some time to fix."

Hammond: "All right, let's get some extra hands from the flight deck to help out."

Before Ishimura, they would need to fix Kellion first.
As their directive start off in requiring manpower,
Kendra performs a standard operating procedure.
Keeping track of their status throughout the mission.

Kendra: "Hold still, Isaac, I'm synching up everyone's RIGs with the ship."

Embedded directly into their spine,
The Resource Integration Gear (RIG).
It lights from the bottom, red to blue.
to display the wearers' overall health.

Used for monitoring vitals of the aged.
Reducing clutter and machinery,
as it can administer medications, too.
Injecting drugs straight to vertebrae.

Seeing other potential uses of the RIG,
its market expanded to other sectors.
From workers, military and scientists.
Vastly altered since its conception,
Its purpose still relatively the same.

Kendra: "Okay, we're done. Clean bill of health for everyone."

Hammond: "All right. We've still got a job to do. We're moving out."

The other crew members walked passed him,
Corporals Johnston and Chen, Hammond, Kendra.
He stood and waited 'til each one have exited the ship,
They couldn't help but notice his peculiarities.

His helmet covers the entirety of his head,
appendages portruding from jaw to his face.
Completely of metal, emitting a glimpse of light.
Giving the impression of a machine more than a man.

A standard issue uniform that has seen better days.
The insulation layer had scratches, burns, and poked holes
while the armor is on its early stage of rust and discoloration.
A mark of a person who had worked tirelessly for years.

Being neglected by the company is not uncommon,
but nevertheless eliciting a polarized reaction.
A commendation for loyalty and dedication?
Or pity that their welfare's unworthy of preservation?

For Isaac, though, these opinions were irrelevant.
As long as something works, he maximizes its use.
The tools he would need on a daily basis are on his person.
And if they weren't, he would construct them himself.

This is reflected in the minimalist nature of his RIG.
Aside from detecting one's deteriorating health,
its key feature is that it can project holograms.

And just like any other device that has a screen,
a plethora of applications were developed for it.
Along with the built-in messenger for every media,

he has an inventory tracking application,

a 3D wireframe mapping of his current location,

database to save and review his received digital logs,

and a Journal to organize prioritized missions.

As he pulled up the RIG's holographic interface,
parts of the cockpit closest to him lit up as well.
He takes note of his next objective-
To follow the others outside and look for help.

Upon setting foot inside the USG Ishimura,
a sharp cold air amidst the fire kicked in.
Eerie echoes reverberating a large empty hangar,
some might say that the ship is calling out a warning.

Kendra: "You didn't lose power to the port booster. You LOST the port booster! Unbelievable."

Furious as she examine their predicament,
Kendra still thought there would've been a better way.
One that doesn't involve destroying half the shuttle,
and probably along with it, their ticket back home.

They continued walking down the bridge.
Suddenly, the large television turned on by itself.
An automatic greeting for newly arrived employees.
They've triggered the first sensor onboard Ishimura.

Transcript: Welcome CEC employee, to the USG Ishimura. The Ishimura was the first Planet Cracker-class vessel and is now the oldest serving, with sixty-two years of continuous service. It holds the record for highest number of planet cracks, with thirty-four to date and highest dead weight of refined product at fourteen trillion kilotons and counting. Whether you'll be joining us aboard the Ishimura or strengthening the extraction work force on the colony, the captain and crew would like to welcome you and we hope your stay is a pleasant one. Concordance Extraction Company: "Powering Humanity into the Future"

Looking upwards from the television playback,
the inspiring signage of the USG Ishimura, glowing brightly.
Each of its letter scaled so it spans from one end to another.
Its grandiose persisting against the old, dilapidated remainder.

Aptly named after Hideki Ishimura,
the creator of the ShockPoint Drive.
They say it translates "rock village".
Commemoration consistent with purpose.

JACOB CAJIPO, 2025
