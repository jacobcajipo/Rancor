The limit

Even with intense contingent drills and simulations, the human body has a fixed gravitational force threshold.

A sustained or rapid shifting will constrict blood vessels, effectively halting oxygen supply to the brain.

The first to go haywire is the visual faculty, straining every little vein that goes around the eyes.

Followed by bruising in random parts as they bleed inside. Some survived to defy this limit, but none came out unscathed.

Each a veteran on interstellar travel, the crew of the Kellion made a quick recovery.

Awoken by the sound of cracking electricity, they outmaneuvered death only by a millisecond.
