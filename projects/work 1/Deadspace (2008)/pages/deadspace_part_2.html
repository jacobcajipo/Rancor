The limit

Even with intense contingent drills and simulations,
the human body has a fixed gravity force threshold.
A sustained or rapid shifting will constrict blood vessels,
effectively halting oxygen supply to the brain.

The first to go haywire is the visual faculty,
straining every little vein that goes around the eyes.
Followed by bruising in random parts as they bleed inside,
None survive to defy this limit and lived to tell it.

Each of them, veteran on interstellar travel,
The crew of the Kellion had a fast recovery.
Awoken by the sound of cracking electricity,
they outmaneuvered death only by a millisecond.

Hammond: Is everyone okay?

Kendra: What...? What the hell were you thinking?! Were you trying to get us killed?

Hammond: I just saved our asses, Ms. Daniels! If we had aborted at that speed and distance, we would have smashed right into the side of the Ishimura. Now settle down, and let's get to work. Corporal, report!

While Kendra and Hammond kept bickering in the foreground,
the blast shields retract, showing the insides of a hangar.
Isaac put on his helmet and got ready to work,
painfully aware of their shuttle's demise from the crash.

Chen: I'm not getting any readings from the port booster and we've lost comms and autopilot. It'll take some time to fix.

Hammond: All right, let's get some extra hands from the flight deck to help out.

Before Ishimura, Kellion would need fixing first.
As their directive start off requiring manpower,
Kendra performs standard operating procedure.
Keeping record of their status throughout the mission.

Kendra: Hold still, Isaac, I'm synching up everyone's RIGs with the ship.

Embedded straight to their spine,
The Resource Integration Gear (RIG).
It lights from the bottom, red to blue,
For displaying the overall health.

Used to view vitals of the aged,
reducing cords and machinery.
Administers medications, too.
Injecting the drugs to vertebrae.

Seeing other potential uses,
its market expanded to sectors.
Workers, military, scientists,
significantly improved it since.

Kendra: Okay, we're done. Clean bill of health for everyone.

Hammond: All right. We've still got a job to do. We're moving out.

Isaac got a good look at the crew as they passed him,
Corporals Johnston and Chen, Hammond, and lastly Kendra.
He stood and waited 'til each one have exited the ship,
They couldn't help but notice his peculiarities.

A helmet that covers the entirety of his head,
appendages portruding from his jaw towards his face.
Completely of metal, emitting a glinting blue light.
Giving the impression of a machine more than a man.

Standard issue uniform that has seen better days.
The insulation had scratches, burnt scars, poked holes.
Armor on early stage of rust discoloration.
A mark of a person who had worked tirelessly for years.

Its common to be forgotten by a corporation.
Nevertheless, elicits a polarized reaction.
A commendation for loyalty and dedication?
Or pity their welfare's unworthy of preservation?

For Isaac, though, these opinions were irrelevant.
As long as something works, he maximizes its usage.
The tools he would need on a daily are on his person.
If he misses one, he'd construct from heaps of spare parts.

This reflects in the minimalist nature of his RIG.
Aside from detecting wearer's deteriorating health,
its key feature's projecting interactive holograms.
Plethora of applications were developed for it.

He installed an inventory tracking application,

a 3D wireframe mapping of his current location,

database storing all his received digital logs,

and a Journal to organize prioritized missions.

As he pulled up the hologram interface from his wrist,
parts of the cockpit in vicinity lit up with it.
He takes note of his next objective into his Journal-
Follow the others outside and begin looking for help.

Upon setting foot inside USG Ishimura,
sharp cold air amidst the heated flames wafted around him.
Eerie echoes reverberating an empty hangar,
listening close, uncanny resemblance of a warning.

Kendra: You didn't lose power to the port booster. You LOST the port booster! Unbelievable.

Furious as she examine the ship's predicament,
Kendra still thought there would've been a better method.
One that doesn't involve destroying half of the shuttle,
and probably along with it, their ticket way back home.

They continued walking along the bridge to the lobby.
Suddenly, the large television turned on by itself.
Automatic greeting for newly arrived employees.
They've triggered the first sensor onboard the planetcracker.

Aptly named after Hideki Ishimura,
the creator of the ShockPoint Drive.
They say it translates to "rock village".
Commemoration aligned with purpose.

Looking upwards from where the television is playing,
The iconic plaque of the ship, glowing valiantly.
Each letter is scaled up so it spans from left to right.
Its grandiose persisting in the dilapidation.

Arriving at the lobby, he caught up with the others.
Whole place was dark, with only the emergency lights on.
The door was supposed to open at close proximity,
but it seems the mechanism is inoperable.

Hammond: Guess the power's down everywhere. Isaac, get over here and hack the door pad.

As engineer, Isaac has admin priveleges to security.
He can override protocols and pry open the doors.
Provided that the door pads have a sufficient power,
it can be done by using his RIG's interface and a click.

Like an abandoned residence, the flight lounge was pitch black.

relieved by humming lightbulbs turning on in disarray,
