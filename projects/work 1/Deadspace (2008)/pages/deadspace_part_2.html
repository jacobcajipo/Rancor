The limit

Even with intense contingent drills and simulations, the human body has a fixed gravitational force threshold.

A sustained or rapid shifting will constrict blood vessels, effectively halting oxygen supply to the brain.

The first to go haywire is the visual faculty, straining every little vein that goes around the eyes.

Followed by bruising in random parts as they bleed inside. Some survived to defy this limit, but none came out unscathed.

Each a veteran on interstellar travel, the crew of the Kellion made a quick recovery.

Awoken by the sound of cracking electricity, they outmaneuvered death only by a millisecond.

Hammond: Is everyone okay?

Kendra: What...? What the hell were you thinking?! Were you trying to get us killed?

Hammond: I just saved our asses, Ms. Daniels! If we had aborted at that speed and distance, we would have smashed right into the side of the Ishimura.

Now settle down, and let's get to work. Corporal, report!

The two officers kept on with their bickering. The blast shields retracted, showing the insides of a hangar.

Isaac put on his helmet and got ready to work, painfully aware of their shuttle's demise from the crash.

Chen: I'm not getting any readings from the port booster and we've lost comms and autopilot. It'll take some time to fix.

Hammond: All right, let's get some extra hands from the flight deck to help out.

Before Ishimura, Kellion would need fixing first.

As their directive starts off requiring manpower, Kendra performs standard operating procedure. Keeping record of their status throughout the mission.

Kendra: Hold still, Isaac, I'm synching up everyone's RIGs with the ship.

Embedded straight to their spine,
The Resource Integration Gear (RIG)
It lights from the bottom, red to blue,
For displaying the overall health.

Used to view vitals of the aged,
reducing cords and machinery.
Administers medications, too.
Injecting the drugs to vertebrae.

Seeing other potential uses,
its market expanded to sectors.
Workers, military, scientists,
significantly improved it since.

Kendra: Okay, we're done. Clean bill of health for everyone.

Hammond: All right. We've still got a job to do. We're moving out.

Isaac got a good look at the crew as they passed him, Corporals Johnston and Chen, Hammond, and lastly Kendra.

He stood and waited 'til each one exited the ship, they couldn't help but notice his peculiarities.

A helmet that covers the entirety of his head,
appendages portruding from his jaw towards his face.

Completely of metal, emitting a steady blue light.
Giving the impression of a machine more than a man.

Standard issue uniform that has seen better days.
The insulation had scratches, burnt scars, and poked holes.

Armor in the early stage of rust discoloration.
A mark of a person who had worked tirelessly for years.

Nothing new to be forgotten by a corporation.
Nevertheless, elicits a polarized reaction.

A commendation for loyalty and dedication?
Or pity their welfare's unworthy of attention?

For Isaac, though, these opinions were irrelevant. As long as something works, he maximizes its usage.

The tools he would need on a daily basis are on his person. If he misses one, he'd construct from heaps of spare parts.

This reflects in the minimalist nature of his RIG.

Aside from detecting wearer's deteriorating health, its key feature's projecting interactive holograms.

Plethora of applications were developed for it.

As he pulled up the hologram interface from his wrist, parts of the cockpit in the vicinity lit up with it.

He installed an inventory tracking application,

a 3D wireframe mapping of his current location,

database storing all his received information,

and a Journal to organize objectives and missions.

He can access these applications at any time, without having to pause so he could see what's happening.

For now, he has to catch up with the others. They were already walking along the bridge to the lobby.

Upon setting foot inside USG Ishimura,
sharp cold air amidst the heated flames wafted.

Eerie echoes reverberating an empty hangar,
listening closely, resemblant of a dying animal.

Kendra: You didn't lose power to the port booster. You LOST the port booster! Unbelievable.

Furious as she examine the ship's predicament,
Kendra still thought there would've been a better way.

One that doesn't involve destroying half of the shuttle,
and probably along with it, their ticket way back home.

They continued treading down the bridge at a pace.
Still, they tripped a sensor that detects movements...

causing the large television to turn on by itself.
An automated greeting for newly arrived employees.

Looking upwards from where the television is playing, the gazing nameplate of the ship, glowing valiantly.

Each letter is scaled up so it spans from left to right. Its grandiose rebelling against the dilapidation.

Aptly named after Hideki Ishimura,
the creator of the ShockPoint Drive.

They say it translates to "rock village".
Commemoration aligned with purpose.

Having caught up with the others, thus another hurdle.
Whole place was dark, with only the emergency lights on.

The door was supposed to open at close proximity
but the sensor mechanism is inoperable.

Hammond: Guess the power's down everywhere. Isaac, get over here and hack the door pad.

As engineer, Isaac has admin privileges to security. He can override protocols and pry open the doors.

Provided that the door pads have sufficient power, it can be done by using his RIG's interface and a click.

Unexpectedly reminiscent of an abandoned house,
a fine layer of dust accumulated on the stained floor.

People are supposed to be waiting for them in this lounge.
Frozen with anxiety, it took him a while to snap back.

Kendra: Seems like everyone was trying to pack in a hurry.

Hammond: There should be a security detail here.

Kendra: Yeah, well there's not. There's nobody here. I can't pick up any broadcasts.

It’s obvious that multiple personnel came this way.
Numerous luggages on the seats left unattended.

The prompter tells that all flights were cancelled.
If no one was allowed to get out, then where are they now?

Hammond: That security console is still live. Isaac, log in and see what you can find. Kendra, get that elevator back online.

Kendra: Power's dead. I can't.

Hammond: Then re-route the damn power! Look, if we all cooperate, we can figure this out a lot sooner.

Hammond: Let's get that computer display up, Isaac.

When Hammond delegated the tasks with authority, there was a sliver where he almost lost composure.

Kendra de-escalated the tension, turning her back. It’s clear that Isaac wasn’t the only one on edge.

Soon as the door to the console opened, his chest tightened.
He took a second glance for good measure until he's certain.

A nauseating puddle of blood,
some splattered across the walls.

Someone died in this cold, dark room.
The absent corpse failed to ease him.

With a deep breath and clenched teeth,
he carefully pushed himself onwards.

Pictures of death with each step,
Couple of strides feeling like a mile.

His reward, a fully functional terminal to interface.
Unfortunately, they wouldn’t like what they’re about to see.

Johnston: Huh. That doesn't look good. She's taken a lot of damage.

Hammond: The tram system's offline. Getting around is going to be difficult.

Hammond: The air seems to be flowing again. That's a start.

The unrelenting blare of the alarm caught them off guard.
It’s hard to remain calm with shock and trauma still fresh.

Kendra: "What the hell was that?"

Hammond: "Automatic quarantine must have tripped when the filtration system restarted. Everybody relax..."

Something beyond a logical explanation happens,
There are voices coming right out of the walls and ceiling.

Kendra: "What was that? Did you hear that?!"

Hammond: "I'm not sure..."

CRASH

Johnston: "What the hell?"

flop

Kendra: "I don't know! Something's in the room with us!"

A figure emerged from where the sound originated.

It was bleeding profusely, curled up but facing the floor.

With pale skin stretching across its back like rubber.
It almost looked human...

but then it stood up.

An abnormally elongated limb with a sharp end.
Reached out to Corporal Johnston in the blink of an eye.

Blood gushed from his neck and spread on to the glass windows.

His head fell off and rolled away from his impaled body.
