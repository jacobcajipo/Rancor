The Dark

Humans normally dread the unknown lurking in the dark.
Shunning it ensured their survival for generations.
For those whose curiosity must be sated, enthralled.
They would pursue the endeavor even if it kills them.

Fear and defiance would drive them to explore the stars.

At the cusp of a technological evolution,
came the invention of a device called the ShockPoint Drive.
This had enabled travelling in speeds faster than light.
The answer to the greatest question, now within their reach.

Yet all that they got was frustration and disappointment.
For there was absolutely no other living beings.
A galaxy that's nothing more than an empty promise.
The discovered habitable worlds, strangely barren.

The wanderlust inevitably lost its novelty.
They have been neglected, abandoned, utterly alone.
It's replaced by restlessness, questioning their purpose.
Why did their cruel gods created them in the first place?

As Isaac Clarke was ruminating about his death,
the excruciating pain that's demanding to be felt.
A thought that even a sturdy metallic shuttle,
shudders, subjected to the unforgiving universe.

will his fragile mind and body
endure the horrors hereafter?
