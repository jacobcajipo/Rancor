The Dark

Mankind had always been afraid of the dark,
be it the unknown, the void, the abyss, the shadows.
Steering away had ensured their survival for millennia,
But some are drawn into it, piqued by their curiosity.

This fear is also what drove them to explore the stars.

At the cusp of technological evolution,
came the invention of the ShockPoint Drive.
This enabled travelling in speeds faster than light itself.
Now, the answer to their great question is within reach.

Yet all they found was disappointment.
For there was nothing out there. Not one.
The galaxies were emptier than they look.
Trillions of stars with barren lifeless planets.

The once feared unknown quickly lost its novelty,
they have been utterly alone for who knows how long.
It was replaced by an uneasiness, a lack of purpose.
Did their god created them just to die alone?

As Isaac Clarke was ruminating about his own death,
he thought about excruciating pain, the suffering and torment.
A realization that even a titanium shuttle like USG Kellion,
shudders as it’s subjected to the unforgiving universe.

Is he, made of nothing but flesh and bone,
even meant to know what lies beyond?

Chapter 1: New Arrivals

With eyes closed, Isaac managed to block out the noise.
There was a constant screeching of metal against metal,
yet he finds it almost comforting.
For him, it was always like this.

But the light managed to find its way.
His view was hijacked by a machine-generated list.
A handful of names with even fewer directives.
Judging from the premise, simple routine maintenance.

CEC MISSION ER529 - STATUS UPDATE... -
USG KELLION EN ROUTE TO AEGIS SYSTEM -
PRIMARY MISSION ASSETS... -
...ISAAC CLARKE - ENGINEERING AND SHIP SYSTEMS SPECIALIST...
...KENDRA DANIELS - COMPUTER SPECIALIST...
...ZACH HAMMOND - CHIEF SECURITY OFFICER...

DIRECTIVE A: LOCATE MINING VESSEL USG ISHIMURA...
DIRECTIVE B: DIAGNOSE AND REPAIR COMMUNICATIONS BLACKOUT...
TIME TO CONTACT: 3 MINUTES…

Counting in the two pilots, makes a total of five.
Isaac volunteered to be a part of the crew.
They were more than happy to have him on board,
perhaps no one in their right mind would do so for free.

He already had theories on the problem at hand.
Could be a busted communications array but not likely.
A total blackout never happened before.
After all, these Planetcrackers were built to last.

But love for work wasn't his motive.
Few days prior, he got a message.
A disturbing video log from Nicole Brennan,
Isaac's long term partner.

Nicole: "Isaac, it's me. I wish I could talk to you. I'm sorry. I'm sorry about everything. I wish I could just talk to someone."

Since Nicole got accepted as a Senior Medical Officer,
she'd been communicating with Isaac from the USG Ishimura.
For him, she seemed happy with her new job,
despite the heavy workload placed upon her.

Nicole Brennan: "It's all falling apart here. I can't believe what's happening... It's strange... such a little thing..."

The message was a garbled mess.
They haven't fought in a long time,
but she kept apologizing anyway.

Isaac knew, something terrible had happened.

Kendra Daniels: - "How many times have you watched that thing? Guess you really miss her. Don't worry, we're almost there. You'll be able to look her up once we're onboard. Sounds like you two have a lot of catching up to do."

A voice interrupted his train of thought.
Kendra Daniels, the computer specialist.
She's kind enough to check on him,
he was about to respond when-

As if being expelled from the rabbit hole,
they have arrived at their destination.
The pilots synched with the orbit to stabilize.
A mesmerizing yet haunting vista was revealed.

Scattered debris occupy the outer atmostphere,
huge rocks with a size that can topple entire cities,
and one colossal planet-

Broken.

The Aegis VII. -

The seventh planet of the Cygnus system.
Its surface covered with a golden orange hue,
obstructed only by the massive void on its side.

Kendra: "All this trouble over that chunk of rock."

The first words Kendra thought of after seeing it
sounded like she was talking about a piece of junk.
Not that it was meant to offend,
but an attempt to initiate rapport.

For Zach Hammond, the Chief Security Officer,
it was blatant arrogance that needed correcting.
One that he felt obliged to step in.
Hinting his reverence for the work.

Hammond: - "Deep space mining is a lucrative business, Ms. Daniels. Aegis 7 is a gold mine, according to prospector's reports. Cobalt, Silicon, Osmium...

Mining said raw materials on Earth used to be a process.
The Concordance Extraction Corporation (CEC)
had worked on a solution for years to expedite this.
They claim it is for the benefit of all mankind.

Gone are negotiating with locals before mining.
So too are the paperwork and bureaucracy.
No one bats an eye if they never lived there.
Finding a vein was an impossible task in and of itself.

For there are thousands of known planets devoid of life,
with billion others just waiting to be surveyed for prospect.

Except that a pearl wouldn't just reveal itself.
Cruel methods were a necessity for efficiency,
or that's what the CEC led themselves to believe.
And so they built their own tools of destruction.

Hammond: "Now, where is she? There she is. We have visual contact."

Kendra: "So that's the Ishimura? Impressive..."

Planetcrackers.

Gigantic ships with a singular purpose.
To break open entire moons and planets,
then piece by piece, tear at its crust and mantle,
extract all the resources within until it's dry.

Hammond: "The USG Ishimura. Biggest Planetcracker in her class. And it looks like they already popped the cork."

The USG Ishimura was the first of its class,
the crowning achievement of the CEC,
It served tirelessly for almost 60 years.
Now only a shadow of its former self.

Kendra: "Why is it all dark? I don't see any running lights."

Hammond: "Corporal, take us in closer and hail them. And stay clear of the debris field. We're here to fix their ship, not the other way around."

Corporal Chen: "USG Ishimura, this is the emergency maintenance team of the USG Kellion responding to your distress call. Come in Ishimura."

As the unresponsive Ishimura drew near,
Isaac was filled with a sense of dread,
there was nothing he could do but watch,
as its shadow slowly engulf the shuttle.

The crew anxiously searched for any signs of life.
They took turns to garner a response from the radio,
still nothing.

Kendra: "You're going to need to boost the signal if their power is low."

Hammond: "Yes, we know. Boost the signal. More.

Kendra: "Never heard of a total communications blackout on one of these things. You'd think with a thousand people on board, someone would pick up the phone--"


A strange audio made it through,
They all strained to make sense of it.
It was someone's voice with...
something else.

Chen: "What is that?"

Kendra: "It's a busted array like we thought. Sounds like they're having problems with their encoder. You get us down there and Isaac and I can fix it. Forty-eight hours max."

Kendra said the same thing as Isaac had theorized,
A busted array that cascaded into the encoders.
It would take two gruelling days of work to fix,
so he needs to see Nicole before anything else.

Hammond:
"Alright, you heard the lady.
Take us in. Let's see what needs fixing."

Corporal Johnston:
"Gravity tethers engaged.
Automatic docking procedure is go."

CRASH

Chen: "What the hell?"

The USG Kellion was rocked by a sudden blast,
so strong that it sent them off-track.
Whatever hit them was hurling at a speed,
they would need to think even faster.

Johnston: "Sir! The autodock... "

Hammond: "What is it?"

Chen: "We're off track! We're going to hit the hull!"

They've came a long way,
just to die a gruesome death.
Their thoughts, ideals, regrets, bodies,
dismembered in the vacuum of space.

Such a waste of life.

Hammond: "Hit the blast shields! Their guidance tether is damaged! Switch to manual. NOW!"

Kendra: "Inside the magnetic field? Are you insane?! Abort!"

Hammond: "No! We can make it inside! Corporal, I gave you an order!"

Chen: "Damn, the field's too strong!"

WOOSH
