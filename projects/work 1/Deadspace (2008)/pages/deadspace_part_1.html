The Dark

Mankind had always been afraid of the dark,
be it the unknown, the void, the abyss, the shadows.
Steering away had ensured their survival for millennia,
but some are drawn into it, piqued by their curiosity.

This fear and defiance drove them to explore the stars.

At the cusp of technological evolution,
came the invention of the ShockPoint Drive.
This enabled travelling in speeds faster than light.
The answer to the greatest question, within reach.

Yet all they found was disappointment.
For there was nothing out there. Not one.
The galaxies were emptier than they look.
Countless habitable worlds, strangely barren.

The once feared unknown quickly lost its novelty.
They have been utterly alone after all this time.
It was replaced by an uneasiness, a lack of purpose.
Did their god created them just to die alone?

As Isaac Clarke was ruminating about his own death,
he thought of excruciating pain, demanding to be felt.
A realization that even a titanium shuttle like USG Kellion,
shudders as it’s subjected to the unforgiving universe.

Is he, made of nothing but flesh and bone,
even meant to know what lies beyond?

Chapter 1: New Arrivals

With eyes closed, Isaac managed to block out the noise.
There was a constant screeching of metal against metal,
It felt suffocating, yet somehow weirdly comforting.
Perhaps after years of employment, he'd grown used to it.

The blue light still managed to find its way to him.
His vision was hijacked by a machine-generated list:
The mission, a handful names, and even fewer directives.
Judging from the premise, a simple routine maintenance.

Including the two pilots, makes a total of five.

They were more than happy to have him on board,
no one in their right mind would do so for free.

The seasoned engineer he was, he worked on some theory.
Only theories, since all assumptions seem far-fetched.

After all, Planet Cracker - Class ships were built to last.

But love for work wasn't his motive for being here.
Few days prior these events, he received a message.

Isaac's partner who's working for the same company he is.

Nicole: "Isaac, it's me. I wish I could talk to you. I'm sorry. I'm sorry about everything. I wish I could just talk to someone."

From his point of view she seemed happy with her new job,
despite the heavy workload being placed upon her.

Nicole Brennan: "It's all falling apart here. I can't believe what's happening... It's strange... such a little thing..."

Isaac just couldn't shrug off the bad feeling,
