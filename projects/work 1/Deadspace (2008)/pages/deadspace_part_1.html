Dead Space

Chapter 1: New Arrivals

With eyes still closed, Isaac managed to block out the noise.
There's a constant screeching of metal against metal.

It’s suffocating but somehow he finds it oddly comforting.
Perhaps after years of employment, he'd grown used to it.

A blue light, however, managed to find its way to him.
His vision, hijacked by a machine-generated list.

The mission, a few names, and even fewer directives.
Judging from the premise, a simple routine maintenance.

CEC MISSION ER529
STATUS UPDATE...
USG KELLION EN ROUTE TO AEGIS SYSTEM...
PRIMARY MISSION ASSETS...
  ...ISAAC CLARKE - ENGINEERING AND SHIP SYSTEMS SPECIALIST...
  ...KENDRA DANIELS - COMPUTER SPECIALIST...
  ...ZACH HAMMOND - CHIEF SECURITY OFFICER...
DIRECTIVE A: LOCATE MINING VESSEL USG ISHIMURA...
DIRECTIVE B: DIAGNOSE AND REPAIR COMMUNICATIONS BLACKOUT...
TIME TO CONTACT: 3 MINUTES…

Including the two pilots, would make a total of five.
Isaac volunteered himself to be part of their crew.

They were beyond delighted to accept his offering,
perhaps one would've been insane to work without a fee.

The seasoned engineer he is, he worked on some theory.
And only theories, since all assumptions are far-fetched.

Because a total blackout had never happened before.
After all, these Planet Cracker - Class ships were built to last.

Love for his career wasn't his motive for being here.
Few days prior to these events, he had received a message.

An incomplete, concerning video log from Nicole,
former co-worker at the corporation, now partner.

Earning the job as Medical Officer months ago,
she had been in contact aboard the Ishimura.

From his standpoint, she's satisfied with her new title.
Despite the heavier workload being placed upon her.

Nicole: It's all falling apart here. I can't believe what's happening... It's strange... such a little thing...

Which made the circumstances a shocking development.
Isaac just couldn't shrug off an estranged undertone.

A hunch that something terrible had already happened.
He would need to find her fast, and hope to get an answer.

How many times have you watched that thing? Guess you really miss her. Don't worry, we're almost there.

You'll be able to look her up once we're onboard. Sounds like you two have a lot of catching up to do.

A calm voice sweeped in, soothing his worries.
It's Kendra Daniels, the crew's computer specialist.

Very mindful of her to be checking up on others,
he was about to respond to her queries, suddenly-

Scattered debris littering the outer atmostphere,
huge rocks with a size that can topple developed cities.

One colossal planet at the mercy of colonists,
battered, broken, fragmented beyond recognition.

The Aegis VII.
It's the seventh planet of the Cygnus Star system.
The surface covered with a distinct golden orange hue,
obstructed only by the massive void on its far side.

Kendra: All this trouble over that chunk of rock.

The first words Kendra thought of upon seeing the Aegis,
sounded like she was talking about a scrapped junk piece.

Though her intention wasn't to offend anybody,
She could just be attempting to initiate rapport.

Zach Hammond, the Chief Security Officer, ticked off.
It was blatant arrogance that needed correcting.

One that he felt obliged to step in and explain himself.
Hint of his reverent and rigid personality.

Hammond: Deep space mining is a lucrative business, Ms. Daniels. Aegis 7 is a gold mine, according to prospector's reports. Cobalt, Silicon, Osmium...

Mining for materials on Earth used to be a process.
The Concordance Extraction Corporation (CEC),

had worked on a solution for years to expedite this.
They claim that it is for the benefit of all mankind.

For there's a hundred known planets devoid of any life,
and thousand others waiting to be surveyed for prospect.

Hammond: Now, where is she? There she is. We have visual contact.

Kendra: So that's the Ishimura? Impressive...

Planet Cracker - Class Vessels

Gigantic ships built with a singular purpose in mind.
To break open entire moons and planets to submission,
and then piece by piece, tear away at its crust and mantle.

When all resources are extracted, they're left discarded.

Hammond: The USG Ishimura. Biggest Planet Cracker in her class. And it looks like they already popped the cork.

The USG Ishimura was the first of its kind,
used to be the crowning achievement of the CEC.

It has served tirelessly for almost 62 years now.
A miracle that it held out for as long as it did.

Kendra: Why is it all dark? I don't see any running lights.

Hammond: Corporal, take us in closer and hail them. And stay clear of the debris field. We're here to fix their ship, not the other way around.

Corporal Chen: USG Ishimura, this is the emergency maintenance team of the USG Kellion responding to your distress call. Come in Ishimura.

The crew anxiously searched for any signs of life on board.
They took turns to garner a response from the radio.

Kendra: You're going to need to boost the signal if their power is low.

Hammond: Yes, we know. Boost the signal. More.

Kendra: Never heard of a total communications blackout on one of these things. You'd think with a thousand people on board, someone would pick up the phone--

A bizarre audio slipped in.
They all strained hard to make sense of it.

Chen: What is that?

Kendra: It's a busted array like we thought. Sounds like they're having problems with their encoder. You get us down there and Isaac and I can fix it. Forty-eight hours max.

Hammond: Alright, you heard the lady. Take us in. Let's see what needs fixing.

Corporal Johnston: Gravity tethers engaged. Automatic docking procedure is go.

CRASH

Chen: What the hell?

The USG Kellion was rocked by a sudden blast,
on a magnitude sending them to a rapid freefall.
Whatever hit them was hurling at high velocity,
they need to think faster to get back on trajectory.

Johnston: Sir! The autodock...

Hammond: What is it?

Chen: We're off track! We're going to hit the hull!

They have come a long way,
to die a gruesome death.
Their last thoughts and bodies,
dismembered in space.

Such a waste of life.

Hammond: Hit the blast shields! Their guidance tether is damaged! Switch to manual. NOW!

Kendra: Inside the magnetic field? Are you insane?! Abort!

Hammond: No! We can make it inside! Corporal, I gave you an order!

Chen: Damn, the field's too strong!

THUD
